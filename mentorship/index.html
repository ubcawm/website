Mentorship & Community

Association for Women in Mathematics fosters a culture of support, mentorship and a sense of community.

Grow Together

Whether you are in your first year trying to figure out which courses to take, or a senior student looking to give back — there is a place for you in our mentorship community.

We believe that the best way to learn mathematics is with peers who are going through the same thing.

Who Is This For?

Any female-identifying UBC student interested in mathematics is welcome — regardless of year, program, or experience level. Mentors are typically upper-year undergrads, graduate students, or alumni.

When Does It Run?

The mentorship program runs annually. Applications open at the start of each academic year. Spots are limited so sign up early!

The Mentorship Program

How It Works

Our mentorship program runs annually, matching upper-year undergrads and graduate students with newer members of the math community. Pairs are matched based on academic interests, career goals, and availability.

Mentors commit to meeting with their mentee at least twice a month, providing guidance on course selection, research pathways, and life in the math department. All skill levels and backgrounds are welcome.

Applications open at the start of each academic year. Spots are limited — sign up early!

Community Initiatives

Peer Mentorship Program

Upper-year students are paired with first and second-year students to offer academic guidance, career advice, and a friendly ear throughout the year.

Study Sessions

Weekly drop-in study halls where members can work through problem sets together, share notes, and support one another across courses.

Exam Prep Workshops

Before midterms and finals, AWM hosts targeted review sessions led by top-performing students and teaching assistants.
