Scholarships & Funding

The UBC Mathematics Department awards scholarships based on academic achievement. No application is required for most — the department emails undergraduates when applications open.

Entrance Scholarship in Mathematics

Scholarship

For incoming students from secondary school with exceptional ability in Mathematics. Up to $15,000 total. No application required — awarded on the recommendation of the Department.

Award #04661 Learn More

Stanley M Grant Scholarship in Mathematics

Scholarship

Over $64,000 available for students enrolled in the Department of Mathematics who have completed their first year. Awarded on the recommendation of the Department.

Award #04464 Learn More

Anona Thorne & Takao Tanabe Undergraduate Scholarship

Scholarship

$2,500 annually for outstanding undergraduates in 3rd or 4th year who have declared a Major or Honours in Mathematics. Awarded on recommendation of the Department.

Award #04465 Learn More

Adrian Francis Mulligan Memorial Scholarship

Scholarship

$2,500 annually for an undergraduate student studying Mathematics in the Faculty of Science. Offered by Dr. Gordon Mulligan in memory of his father. Awarded on Department recommendation.

Award #04485 Learn More

Reginald Palliser-Wilson Scholarship

Scholarship

Over $10,500 for students majoring or honouring in Mathematics. Endowed by Joy Gertrude Palmer Helders. Awarded on the recommendation of the Department of Mathematics.

UBC Bursary Program

Conferences & Competitions

Opportunities to present work, hear from leading researchers, and connect with the broader mathematical community.

Putnam Mathematical Competition

Competition

The most prestigious undergraduate math competition in North America. Held annually in December. Top finishers from UBC may be eligible for the Lawrence Roberts Putnam Prize ($250).

Joint Mathematics Meetings (JMM)

Conference

The largest annual gathering of mathematicians in the world, hosted jointly by the AMS and MAA. The AWM holds a workshop specifically for women at JMM each year.

Research Opportunities

Getting involved in research as an undergrad is one of the most valuable things you can do. Here are some ways to get started at UBC.

Funded Research

16 weeks of paid full-time research with a faculty supervisor during the summer. One of the most direct paths into mathematical research. Apply through your supervisor in winter term.
